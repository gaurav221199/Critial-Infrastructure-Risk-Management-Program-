# 2\. Executive summary

During FY2025-26, TMB established and operated an all-hazards CIRMP for its core banking and payments asset, harmonised with APRA CPS 230. No hazard had a significant relevant impact on the asset during the period. The annual assessment identified sixteen material risks across the four hazard vectors, of which two carry a residual High rating - ransomware exposure to the core platform and concentration on a single cloud provider. The most significant program gap is cyber maturity: assessed against the Essential Eight, TMB's overall maturity is currently below the required ML-1 floor, and a funded uplift to ML-2 is underway. All material risks are under active treatment against a 30/90/180-day roadmap, overseen by the Board Risk Committee.

# 4\. Program status and effectiveness

The program was up to date at the end of the reporting period: scope, governance, the risk register and all four hazard modules were current. It was effective in establishing all-hazards risk management and in reducing inherent exposure, but it was not yet fully effective against the cyber framework obligation, where overall Essential Eight maturity sits below ML-1. This is the principal driver of the qualified attestation in section 1 and the focus of the uplift in section 6.

# 5\. Material hazards with significant impact during the period

No cyber, personnel, supply-chain or physical/natural hazard had a significant relevant impact on the availability, integrity, reliability or confidentiality of the asset during FY2025-26. No critical cyber security incidents were reportable to the Australian Signals Directorate during the period. Lower-severity events were managed within tolerance and fed into the risk register.

# 6\. Material variations and planned uplift

The following material variations and planned improvements were identified and are tracked to closure:

- Cyber maturity: uplift the Essential Eight from an overall ML-0 to ML-2 across all eight strategies, prioritising application control and user application hardening (currently ML-0).
- Supply chain: complete cloud exit / portability planning and fourth-party mapping; conduct FOCI and overseas-processing assessments for flagged providers.
- Personnel: introduce a personnel security plan with periodic re-screening, moving toward AusCheck-style background checking.
- Physical & natural: verify geographic separation between the primary and recovery sites and define recovery-time tolerances.

# 7\. Conclusion

TMB has a current, board-governed, all-hazards CIRMP harmonised with CPS 230. The control environment materially reduces inherent risk, and the residual profile is concentrated in two High risks and a known cyber-maturity gap, all under funded remediation. The Board will review progress quarterly and re-attest at the end of FY2026-27.
